Understanding taxation terms

Use the services of the Australian taxation office to gain an understanding of taxation terms relevant to business. The areas you should study include: ABN, TFN, GST, PAYG, FBT, deductions and record keeping.

When you have completed your study check your understanding of the above taxation areas by answering the following ten questions.

At the completed the activity scroll down to check your answers and receive feedback.

Question 1

If your business sells a product to a customer and provides that customer with a tax invoice, the invoice...

a) must include your ABN
b) must not include your ABN
c) may if you choose include your ABN.

 

Question 2

If you have previously been an employee and paid tax and have now commenced your own business as a sole trader…

a) you will have no need for a Tax File Number
b) you must obtain a new Tax File Number
c) you can continue using your existing Tax File Number.

 

Question 3

Goods and services tax (GST) is a broad-based tax on most goods, services and other items sold or consumed in Australia. At what percentage rate (%) is GST applied?

a) 5%
b) 10%
c) 15%
d)100%

 

Question 4

Pay As You Go (PAYG) instalments are

a) only paid if annual turnover exceeds $75000 per year
b) deducted from employee wages
c) monies paid towards end of year tax liability
d) tax amounts returned by the Australian Tax Office.

 

Question 5

You must register for GST if your current or projected turnover is …

a) $25,000 or more
b) $50,000 or more
c) $75,000 or more
d) $100,000 or more.

 

Question 6

Which of the following would be considered a fringe benefit if provided to an employee in addition to their wages?

a) the use of a work car for personal purposes
b) a laptop computer
c) a pair of overalls to provide protection on the job
d) a mobile phone for work purposes.

 

Question 7

When it comes to completing your annual tax return you can include allowable deductions. What are allowable deductions?

a) money spent on personal holidays
b) interest received from investments
c) child care fees
d) expenses incurred in running the business

 

Question 8

Records, such as sales or expense receipts and invoices, are used to calculate your assessable income and allowable deductions. For how long must you keep these records?

a) 1 year
b) 2 years
c) 5 years
d) 10 years

 

 

Question 9

Which of the following is a depreciating asset?

a) land you own
b) a work computer
c) stock you purchase to sell

 

Question 10

If you are operating a business as a sole trader and you employ a worker who is eligible for superannuation guarantee payments, what percentage of their earnings base must you pay into a superannuation fund?

a) a minimum of 9%
b) a maximum of 9%
c) a minimum of 6%
d) a maximum of 6%

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Correct answers

Question 1
Correct choice is (a). The invoice must include the ABN.

Question 2
Correct choice is (c).You can continue using your existing Tax File Number.

Question 3
Correct choice is (b). GST is applied at the rate of 10%.

Question 4
Correct choice is (c). PAYG installments are monies paid towards end of year tax liability.

Question 5
Correct choice is (c). You must register for GST if your current or projected turnover is $75,000 or more.

Question 6
Correct choice is (a). The use of a work car for personal purposes is a fringe benefit.

Question 7
d) Correct, expenses incurred in running the business are allowable deductions

Question 8
Correct choice is (c). You must keep records for a minimum of five years.

Question 9
Correct choice is (b). A work computer can be considered a depreciating asset.

Question 10
Correct choice is (a). You must pay a minimum of 9% of an eligible workers base earnings into a superannuation fund,
