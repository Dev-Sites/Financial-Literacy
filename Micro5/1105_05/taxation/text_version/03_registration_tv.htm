Taxation obligations

Julie - Picture framing

Julie has just started her picture framing business. During this earlier stage she has decided the work will occur in the garage at her home address. Her customers to date have mostly come recommended through word of mouth, but she hopes some recent marketing will increase her business.

Business structure: sole trader
Expected turnover: $48,000
Staff: works alone

Which of the following taxation registrations will Julie have to apply for?

Consider and answer yes or no to the question "Does Julie have to apply for taxation registration for the following five areas".

Answers:

Tax file number

If you answered yes you are incorrect
If prior to commencing business Julie had an individual tax file number, she will not need to register for another. As a sole trader she is able to use her individual tax file number.

If her business structure was a partnership, trust or company, then she would need to register a separate tax file number.

If she does not have an individual tax file number, then she will need to make application to the Australian tax office.

If you answered no you are correct
Correct. Providing Julie has an individual tax file number, there is no need for her to apply for another. A sole trader is able to use their individual tax file number.

 

ABN

If you answered yes you are incorrect
If Julie does not register for GST then she does not have to have an ABN.

She can choose to have an ABN which will make it easier for other tax registrations (e.g. PAYG withholding) as well as avoiding other businesses withholding 46.5% of any payments they make to her. It is a requirement of a business to withhold 46.5% of any payment they make unless they are quoted an ABN.

 

If you answered no you are correct
Correct, if Julie does not register for GST then she does not have to have an ABN.

But she can choose to have an ABN. This will make it easier for other tax registrations (e.g. PAYG withholding) as well as avoiding other businesses withholding 46.5% of any payments they make to her. It is a requirement of a business to withhold 46.5% of any payment they make unless they are quoted an ABN.

 

GST

If you answered yes you are incorrect
Because Julie's expected turnover is less than $75,000 she does not have to register for GST. If this situation changes and she realises her turnover will exceed $75,000 then she has 21 days from this point to register for GST.

Although with an expected turnover of less than $75,000 Julie can, if she wishes, register for GST. By doing so she will have to charge GST and claim GST credits.

 

If you answered no you are correct
Correct, as Julie's expected turnover is less than $75,000 she does not have to register for GST.

Should the situation change and the expectation of turnover rise to in excess of $75,000 she will then have 21 days in which to register.

Although with an expected turnover of less than $75,000 Julie can, if she wishes, register for GST. By doing so she will have to charge GST and claim GST credits.

 

PAYG withholding

If you answered yes you are incorrect
As Julie has no employees she will not have to register for PAYG withholding.

 

If you answered no you are correct
Correct, Julie has no employees so does not need to register for PAYG withholding.

 

Fringe benefits tax

If you answered yes you are incorrect
Julie has no need to register for fringe benefits tax as she has no employees.

Fringe benefits are benefits paid to employees, such as club membership fees, or a work vehicle.

 

If you answered no you are correct
Correct. Julie has no need to register for fringe benefits tax as she has no employees.

Fringe benefits are benefits paid to employees, such as club membership fees, or a work vehicle.

Summary

In Julie's situation there are a number of tax registrations that she can choose to register the business for (i.e. they are not compulsory). These choices are influenced by how she wishes to operate the business.

Julie chooses the following tax registrations

   TFN - As a sole trader Julie can use her existing individual TFN.

   ABN - Although not compulsory, as Julie wishes to register for GST she will need a ABN.

   GST - Julie's expected turnover dictates she does not have to register for GST, but she has decided she prefers to charge GST and claim GST credits.

   PAYG withholding - This is not a requirement as Julie has no employees.

   Fringe benefits tax - This is not a requirement as Julie has no employees.
