Keeping records

Good record keeping is vital for the successful operation of any business. Keeping good records allows helps:

  • determine the financial position of the business
  • provide business details to banks or other stakeholders
  • make it easier to meet your taxation obligations.

Keeping taxation records is a legal requirement. By law, the tax office requires you to keep business records:

  • for five years after they are prepared, obtained or the transactions completed (whichever occurs later)
  • in English, or in a form that can be accessed and understood in order to determine tax liability.

Records can be stored in either paper or electronic form. Penalties exist for not maintaining the required records and for not keeping them for the required time period (five years).

  • Look in the filing cabinet to see some records a business may be required to keep.
  • Select a yellow file tab to open and close the file.

What records to keep? (solo)

The taxation records that a micro business is required to keep differ depending on the business type and structure.

The Australian Taxation Office has developed a Record Keeping Evaluation Tool. This tool can help you work out the records that your business or proposed business should keep.

  • Access the web site of the Australian Taxation Office - www.ato.gov.au.
  • Locate and download the Record Keeping Evaluation Tool.
  • Use the tool to determine the appropriate records that your business should keep.
  • Discuss with your trainer and/or learner group methods of keeping these records.
