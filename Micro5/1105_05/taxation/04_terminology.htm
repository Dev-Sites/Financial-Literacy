Terminology

How well do you understand the terminology used in taxation and taxation requirements?

Whether you are planning to manage your own taxation requirements or use a taxation advisor, you need to understand a number of the common terms used in taxation.

The Australian Taxation Office provides a range of services to assist your understanding of the taxation system. These include:

  • information provided on their web site - www.ato.gov.au
  • a phone service for general enquiries
  • a range of tax guides
  • business seminars and workshops
  • business assistance visits.

Understanding taxation terms (solo)

1. Use the Australian Taxation Office web site to investigate the following areas.

  • What information needs to be included on a tax invoice?
  • What's a tax file number and if you've had one as an employee do you need a new one when you start a business?
  • What's GST and do all business have to register for the GST?
  • What are PAYG instalments and when are they paid?
  • What is fringe benefits tax and when is it paid?
  • What is meant by the term 'allowable deductions' in relation to completing an annual tax return?
  • What business taxation records are needed and how long do you need to keep them?
  • Why is it important to know what a 'depreciating asset' is?
  • How can you meet your superannuation obligations if you are an employer?

2. When you have completed your investigations, see how much you know by completing the following interaction.

  • There are ten questions to answer.
  • Confirm each answer to see whether you are correct.
  • After you've finished the ten questions you will receive a summary.
