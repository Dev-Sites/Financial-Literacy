Types of insurance


Examples of insurance types include:

 
Types of insurance
 Examples
Compulsory

Required by law, for example;

  • work cover
  • motor vehicle third party.
Essential

This covers the largest risks you may encounter, for example;

  • fire
  • consequential loss (loss of profits)
  • public liability
  • burglary.
Very Useful
  • Comprehensive motor vehicle (covers damage to car and other property)
  • Plate glass
  • Personal accident and illness
  • Fidelity guarantee (embezzlement)
  • Marine insurance (not just for ships and cargoes but also for goods sent by air and rail)
  • Money insurance – in transit, on premises, at home (overnight)
  • Property or assets and goods insurance
  • Indemnity insurance – protects against liability for negligence in your work
Other

A micro business can insure against almost any risk but may need to set priorities if working with limited funds.

Insurances worth considering depend on the type of business you are in, for example;

  • Engineering insurance – boiler explosion, machine breakdown
  • Electronic equipment – computer, information loss
  • Book debts cover – inability to recover debts due to destruction of accounting books
  • Export credit – non payment of credit extended to overseas customers
  • Storm and tempest/fusion – sometimes an extension of fire policy
