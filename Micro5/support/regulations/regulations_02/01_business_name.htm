The business name

Selecting a business name is an important step. It will become the identity of your business and may provide a number of benefits including:

  • creating an image for your business
  • informing customers of the nature of your business
  • providing respectability
  • differentiating your business from others
  • increasing the possibility of suppliers offering you discounts
  • being taken more seriously by people
  • a name and associated goodwill that can be sold in the future.

 

Business name requirements

A business name is required for a person carrying on a business under a name that is not their own.

A business may not be carried on under a business name unless it is registered. This does not include a person who carries on business under his or her own personal name or a company name registered with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC).

A business name, which may be registered for a period of 3 years, confers no proprietary interest in the name and does not create a legal person that can enter into legal contract.

You may consider your brand sufficiently valuable to justify protecting your business, company and/ or domain names by registering them as trademarks. A company may trade using its Australian Company Number as a business name. If the company wishes to trade under a different name then it is necessary to register that name as a business name.

 

Registering a business name

The web site www.business.gov.au provides links to all state and territory web sites where you can register a business name.

In addition it also provides a link to the Australian Securities and Investments Commission web site www.asic.gov.au. This site has a feature that allows you to check the company names register for existing names.
