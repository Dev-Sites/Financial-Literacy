Trade practices act

The objective of the Trades Practices Act is to allow the existence of a market place that is fair for all businesses and customers. It covers things such as:

  • unfair trading practices
  • anti-competitive conduct
  • industry codes of practice
  • product safety and labeling
  • price monitoring

 

The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) is responsible for administering the Trades Practices Act.

A booklet to assist you understand your business rights and obligations under the act is available from the ACCC web site.

www.business.gov.au

 

In addition to the Trade Practices Act each each state and territory also has its own fair trading laws, usually referred to as the Fair Trading Act.

Victoria - www.consumer.vic.gov.au

West Australia - www.docep.wa.gov.au
