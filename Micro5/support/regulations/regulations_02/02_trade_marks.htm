Trademarks

The Trade Marks Act protects distinctive marks (e.g. a visual symbol such as a letter, word, phrase, small shape logo, picture, a sound such as an engine roar or a smell such as perfume (scent).

Protection can be acquired by long usage or registration via IP Australia, in any one or more of 42 classes of goods and services. Fees vary, and are payable initially on application and subsequently on registration of the mark. Rights are conferred from the date of the application and apply throughout Australia for a period of 10 years.

Trademark protection enables the holder to prevent others using the same or deceptively similar trademarks.

Without a registered trademark you may need to rely on the Trade Practices Act 1974 or common law for protection.

For details on trade marks visit IP Australia web site www.ipaustralia.gov.au
