Trends example

Trends in the motor vehicle market

The price of petrol is a very political and sensitive issue in Australia, with costs approximately doubling over the last two years.

In Europe, over 70% of all new motor vehicle sales are diesel powered small to large cars.  This trend has occurred because the price of petrol is two to three times the real price in Australia and is very expensive.  Diesel has a similar price to petrol, but most vehicles achieve approximately twice the mileage from a tank of diesel as opposed to petrol.   So, diesel is cost-effective and, now that the performance of most new diesel motor cars is equivalent to that of petrol cars, they are no longer viewed as the 'clanging slugs' of the road.

Recent data in Australia reveals that diesel powered passenger cars in Australia grew 277% in 2007 compared with 2005.  There were 27,050 powered diesel passenger cars sold in Australia in 2007, which represents 4.2% of the total Australian passenger car market sales for 2007.

Some questions for you to think about.

  • What sort of percentage sales do you think diesel cars will achieve in Australia in the year 2015? 
  • Will Australia have diesel power plant options soon?
