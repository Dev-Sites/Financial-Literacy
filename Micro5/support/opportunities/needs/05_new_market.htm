New markets

New markets may come about for various reasons. Frequently a change in technology drives a market change, for example, the mobile digital telephone market followed the analogue mobile phone market, and MP3 and music downloads from the web have followed the traditional CD recorded music format. It's sometimes difficult to work out if a market really is new or is a version of one that already exists.

Documenting the features of new and emerging products/services is important to identify where market gaps may exist for players/competitors within a given market.  Features are usually specific, and measurable, definite issues, as opposed to benefits which are more feelings and sometimes emotions that drive customers to particular brand or business name choices.

Entering a new market has it pros and cons. On the downside, the risk of failure is high. Even if something has worked in a foreign market, it may not be successful in Australia.  The costs of failure can be high in terms of wasted financial resources, time and effort.

On the upside, if it is successful, the first mover can be difficult to overtake by competitors and the financial rewards can be high. Think about the growth in popularity of specialist coffee outlets in Australia over the last 30 years and imagine if you'd been the first one to set up a cafe near a park.
