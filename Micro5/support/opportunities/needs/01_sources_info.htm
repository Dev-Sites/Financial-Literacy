Sources of information

Information can be classified into two main categories:

  • primary
  • secondary.

Primary information

Primary information has been collected from research done specifically for you. Some examples of primary sources of information are:

  • focus group sessions
  • observation of a process
  • informal talks with friends and relatives
  • personal experience
  • personal interviews with potential customers and/or suppliers
  • surveys sent out by post
  • questionnaires put onto an internet site
  • telephone surveys.

Secondary information

Secondary information has been researched and compiled for other purposes, rather than prepared specifically for you. Some examples of secondary sources of information are:

  • business magazines
  • libraries
  • conference and/or seminar notes
  • Austrade
  • Australian Bureau of Statistics
  • banks
  • Business Enterprise Centres
  • marketing consultants
  • government departments
  • local councils
  • industry sources
  • newspapers
  • professional associations
  • shopping centre management
  • trade journals
  • research organisations
  • telephone directories.
