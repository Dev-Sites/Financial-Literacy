Idea or opportunity?

A business idea may come from many different sources:

  • your own imagination
  • your own interests, skills and experience
  • your current or previous employment
  • ideas from visiting shows and exhibitions
  • reading books and magazines
  • talking to friends and associates
  • government research
  • identifying an opportunity within the market for products/services that are not readily available or could be improved
  • listening to people – what they want, what annoys them, etc.

To test if the idea is just a good idea, but not really worth taking it to the next step, find answers to the following questions.

  • Is this a new idea or are others already doing it? If not, why not?
  • Who will buy the product/service? Why? How often?
  • How do you know they will buy the product or use the service?
  • How big is the market?
  • Who are the competitors?
  • What gives you the edge over the competitors?
