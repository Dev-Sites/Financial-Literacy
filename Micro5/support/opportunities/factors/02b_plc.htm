Examining growth and decline

This page examines two of the four stages of the typical product life cycle (PLC) – the growth and decline stages. 

Growth stage

The growth stage of the PLC can be the most attractive to businesses. During the growth stage, businesses usually enjoy higher than expected prices and increased profit per item sold versus other areas of the business. This is due to less competitive pressure compared to mature or declining markets, where there will be more pressure on price and profit margins.

During the growth stage it is good practice to identify and organise information on the market from research using secondary and primary sources and expert interviews.  This information may highlight why the market is growing and predict how long this growth may continue into the future. This is valuable information and can help in minimising risk.

The risk factors associated with growth markets are that a business may need to:

  • extend its range of offerings to customers, with greater costs for  production, marketing, etc
  • consider price reductions due to increasing competition
  • rely heavily on persuasive promotional techniques.

Decline stage

The decline stage of the PLC can be gradual or rapid. The length of time taken for a market to decline will depend on a number of factors such as the market type, size and number of players. Identifying and understanding these issues is important and can assist in successfully predicting the future of declining markets.  Some declining markets may require immediate withdrawal due to risk levels and lack of profit. On the other hand, the market may be large and profitable and the decline so small that withdrawal is not needed.

The risk factors associated with declining markets are that a company may need to:

  • keep the best sellers only in a product range and get rid of many other items or products
  • lower prices to stay competitive
  • rely on promotional efforts that remind customers of the benefits of the products/services.
