Pricing and availability

Two factors that will affect growth and/or attractiveness of markets are:

  • movements in pricing
  • projected changes in the availability of resources. 

Price movements in Australia can be volatile as the economy relies heavily on imports. Trade issues can also affect the prices of both exports and imports.

We have seen some industries where price movements have been steadily upwards, for example, petrol. Other industries show a downward price movement, for example, the price of new motor vehicles as a percentage of average gross earnings in Australia.

Some resources are finite, for example, oil. As finite resources diminish, prices will increase and availability will decrease. So businesses that rely on finite resources must expect pricing and availability variations.

Some resources regenerate on a seasonal basis, for example, crops.  So price is likely to depend on availability.

Other issues that influence price movement and resource availability include:

  • climate change
  • technology
  • pressure groups
  • competition
  • political and legislative change.
