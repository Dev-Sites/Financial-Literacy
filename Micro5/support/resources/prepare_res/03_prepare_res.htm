Monitoring resources

How do you keep an eye on raw materials, consumables and stock to ensure you have enough to meet your needs and your customers' needs?

All businesses need to track the use of resources.

Some businesses use the 'just in time' philosophy and have supplies delivered directly to the production line several times a day to avoid paying for storage space. However, a retail outlet does not look inviting to shoppers if the shelves are empty, so this type of business must store extra stock.

Some essential supplies for your business may be organised as regular orders that arrive on a daily, weekly or monthly basis. For example, a cafe may have a milk and bread order delivered each morning.

A simple way to keep track of stock levels is to have a list showing the name and current quantity of each item. You can then mark off the items you use as they are taken out of storage. When quantities are getting low you can re-order as required. For example, Alison, a hairdresser, has four bottles of shampoo and conditioner left. She uses one bottle of each per working day. It will take two working days for the supplier to deliver the next order. If Alison orders today, even if there is a delivery delay, she should still have enough product to keep working.

If stock is sold, stock levels can be monitored through sales and confirmed through stocktakes. It is important to physically check the quantities of stock to ensure you have enough to keep operating until the next delivery arrives.
