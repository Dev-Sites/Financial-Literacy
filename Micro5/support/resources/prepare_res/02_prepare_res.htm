Maintenance

Regular maintenance should be scheduled for machinery and equipment. Regular maintenance is preferable to repairing breakdowns.

'Planned maintenance' is usually carried out at a specific time to avoid machine downtime during busy periods. Planned maintenance can be either:

  • preventative
  • corrective.

A planned maintenance program can be developed using an equipment or plant register. An equipment or plant register contains all the relevant paperwork, drawings, manufacturers' instructions and other records for all machinery and equipment.

Most planned maintenance, both preventative and corrective, is carried out by trained personnel. This may be someone already working in the business. Or it may be someone contracted from outside the business

Running maintenance is a series of tasks to be done regularly. The aim of running maintenance is to keep the machine and equipment functioning efficiently and safely. It may involve tasks such as cleaning and sanitising equipment, lubricating moving parts, sharpening tools, etc.

Faulty or damaged equipment and machines

Even if regular maintenance is carried out, breakdowns and malfunctions still occur, so it is essential to have procedures in place to handle these situations.

A standard operating procedure for faulty or damaged equipment could be:

  • switch off power if safe to do so
  • clearly tag the machine or equipment
  • contact maintenance supplier
  • remove tag when the machine is ready for use.
