What's a procedure?

A procedure is a set of instructions showing the official or accepted way of doing something. In business, these sets of instructions are often called standard operating procedures (SOPs).

Businesses use procedures to make sure that tasks are completed safely to a required standard. This means that quality, efficiency and time requirements are met without negative personal or environmental effects.

Procedures can be useful for establishing consistent systems for:

  • ensuring workplace safety, for example, cleaning up spills, washing hands
  • installing new resources, for example, a new machine
  • receiving deliveries
  • using resources, for example, how to operate a new photocopier, use a new piece of computer software
  • monitoring the use of resources, for example, tracking the use of consumables such as stationery
  • maintenance of resources, for example, scheduling preventative maintenance on a forklift
  • performing particular processes, for example, ordering and receiving stock and materials.

Sample procedure for receiving deliveries

The following procedure should be followed when receiving any delivery.

  1. All deliveries must be received by a staff member.
  2. Check the invoice against the purchase order.
  3. Check that the invoice includes product codes, descriptions and cost of each item.
  4. Check the contents of each box and, if acceptable, mark off the contents against the invoice.

Staff should not:

  • accept any deliveries during the hours of 12.00 pm and 2.00 pm.
  • allow the carrier to leave before checks are done
  • allow delivery personnel into storerooms or loading areas without supervision
  • accept damaged boxes because the contents may also have been damaged.
