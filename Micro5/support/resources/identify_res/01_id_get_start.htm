Getting started

Different businesses have different needs, so the first step is to determine what's needed for your particular business.

The next step is to work out an order of priority. Some resources will be needed at straight away, while others can wait.

Look at the table to see some common business resources. You can use the table to get your list of resources started.

Identify resources - getting started
Resource Description Examples
Premises This is where you operate your business from and is probably one of the first resources needed. office space, retail shop, home, factory, warehouse
Machinery This refers to large specific  equipment required by your business to manufacture a product or to provide a service. weightlifting machines for a gym, a hoist for a mechanic, an industrial dishwasher for a restaurant
Equipment This is the necessary tools used in your business. It may be purchased, leased or hired depending on your business needs. computers, printers, cleaning appliances, ladders, tables, fridge, personal protective equipment
Materials The supplies that you need to produce a particular product or used to provide a service. lace and buttons to manufacture clothing, an electronic switch for a hairdryer, flour to bake bread
Vehicles Motor vehicles that may be required to operate your business. trucks, cars, forklifts, cherry-pickers, tractors, cranes
Software This is required for office computers and some automated machinery. spreadsheet software for budgeting and tracking finances, photo editing software to crop and trim photographs, word processing software to produce documents
Consumables Goods that are used on a daily basis and need to be replaced regularly. stationery, petrol, toilet paper, coffee, cleaning materials, oil to grease a machine
Human resources These are the people needed to operate your business. Having people with the right skills can help your business meet customer demands. administrative staff, machine operators, drivers, shop assistants, skilled labourers
