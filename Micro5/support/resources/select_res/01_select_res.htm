Choosing suppliers

Finding and choosing reliable and cost competitive suppliers is important to running a successful business.

When negotiating terms of agreement with a new supplier, you can start by asking the following questions.

  • What are the costs and payment terms? For example, overdraft, commercial bill (through the bank), credit card, account.
  • Are there additional costs? For example, taxation, delivery, transport, transaction payments.
  • Are there any discounts offered? For example, for large orders, regular orders.
  • Where is the supplier located? For example, local, interstate, overseas.
  • Can you collect supplies yourself? This can help keep costs down and also let you see how the supplier operates.
  • Does the supplier have sales reps that will come to you? If so, do the reps have good knowledge of the products they sell?
  • What's their history? For example, who else do they supply and for how long?

Reliability and costs

Cost is certainly important when considering suppliers for your business. Generally, the less you pay for raw materials and stock, the greater the potential for higher profits. However if the product supply is not reliable (for example, goods are faulty and/or delivered late) the end result may be loss of customers.

  • Ask other people in your industry about their experience with suppliers, to find out who has a good reputation for supplying reliable quality products on time.
  • Source different suppliers for the same product to compare prices and delivery schedules. You may wish to compare prices from suppliers that are:
    • local
    • interstate
    • overseas.
  • Consider having more than one supplier for critical items (as a backup).

Finding suppliers

You can often find suppliers by checking local telephone directories and by talking to business colleagues.

You can also look for Australian suppliers on the Australian Government Austrade website (opens new window). Search for the Australian Suppliers Directory.
