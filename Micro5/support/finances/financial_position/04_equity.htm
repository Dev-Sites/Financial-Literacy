Personal equity

Before looking elsewhere, consider what funds you are willing and able to put into the business, such as:

  • liquid assets, for example, money in the bank, insurance policies or superannuation that can be cashed in
  • equipment and motor vehicles that could be used in the business. 
 

You could also have your house valued to see what amount you might be able to borrow against it.  If you already have a mortgage on your home, find out the balance of the loan. You can only use your house as security for a subsequent loan with the permission of the mortgager.

Knowing the value of the equity you can put into a business is important. If you need to get financial backing from outsiders, and can show that you're willing to invest an adequate personal contribution, it can demonstrate to them your commitment to the business.

Most lenders insist that the owners contribute a portion of the total amount of money required. The actual amount will depend on the lender's assessment of:

  • how risky the business is
  • the ability of the business to repay the loan with interest
  • the security available
  • qualities of the owner.
