Balance sheet

The balance sheet sets out all the assets and liabilities to show what a business is worth.

Here is an example of a balance sheet.

Text alternative

 

Assets are the items of value held by the business. Some balance sheets classify assets as current and non-current. Current assets include cash and other assets that can quickly be turned into cash. Also things used during the normal operations of a business. Non-current or fixed assets are items retained to support the business activities, rather than used for trading or revenue raising.

Liabilities are amounts owed by the business. Some balance sheets classify liabilities as current and non-current. Current liabilities are amounts owed to outside creditors that are either payable on demand or within a period of not more than a year. Non-current or deferred liabilities are amounts owed that are payable over a relatively long time.

Owner's equity is the owner's residual claim on the assets of the business after all the other liabilities have been settled. Assets less liabilities shows the amount of equity the owner has in the business. The higher the percentage of ownership against debt, the better.

Note: Your accountant is the best person to help you to prepare and analyse your financial statements.
