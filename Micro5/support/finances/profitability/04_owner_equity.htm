Owner's equity

Owner's equity, also known as proprietorship, is the owner's investment in a business.

Owner's equity is calculated by balancing out assets and liabilities. Assets (current and fixed) - liabilities = owner's equity.

For example, a business has current assets of $50,000, fixed assets of $90,000 and liabilities of $60,000. The owner's equity, that is the proportion of total assets owned by the owner, is $80,000.

Comparing Starting and Ending Equity:
Your starting equity is your net ownership of the business.
This is calculated as follows:
Current Assets + Fixed assets – liabilities (loans etc) = Owners Equity
