Financial viability

Assessing viability is essentially based around your Profit and Loss Statement and Balance Sheet for the year.

There are some other ways to look at the financial health of your business.

Current asset ratio

This ratio examines cash flow. The higher the ratio the bettter.

Current asset ratio = Current assets divided by current liabilities.

Return on capital ratio

This ratio shows you the rate of return on your investment.  This is similar to looking at the amount of interest received from a bank on money deposited.

Return on capital ratio = Net profit divided by owner's equity.

Expense ratio

This ratio shows your operating costs as a percentage of sales. This is an indicator of profitability because it highlights the amount of expenditure incurred in earning income.

Expense ratio = Operating expenses divided by sales.

Return on labour

Viability can include return on labour as well as return on capital. What is deemed a satisfactory return can vary from one person to another! For example, one person might feel that working 12-hour days, seven days a week to earn $100,000 is satisfactory, while another person would be dissatisfied.

Note: Your accountant is the best person to help you to analyse the financial viability of a business.
