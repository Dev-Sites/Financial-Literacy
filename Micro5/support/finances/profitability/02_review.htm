Review performance

Keeping accurate and up-to-date records will help a business owner review business performance. For example, at the end of each month, a business owner can total up sales and expenses from the cash books and compare actual performance with forecasts in the cash flow budget. This can identify possible liquidity problems, such as having plenty of sales but no cash, as your debtors have not yet paid for your goods and services. 

Following are some useful question to consider when reviewing business peformance.

  • What has caused this over-performance or under-performance?
  • Is this a one-off situation or is it likely to continue?
  • What implications does this have for my business?
  • Do I need to take corrective action in the under-performing areas?
  • How can I make sure the over-performing areas continue to perform as well as they did?
 

Note: Downward trends that continue over a number of months should be investigated. Seek professional advice to assist you in stabilising the business.
