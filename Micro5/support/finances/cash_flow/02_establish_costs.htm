Establishment costs

Establishment costs include the expenses and asset purchases associated with getting the business started. 

Examples of some establishment costs are:

  • opening stock
  • bonds and initial rent if you are leasing a building or space
  • business name registration
  • incorporation (if becoming a company)
  • bonds for gas or electricity
  • licences to operate
  • council permits
  • fit out of premises
  • stationery
  • tools and equipment
  • loan establishment fee.
 

This list is by no means exhaustive. The type of establishment costs will differ according to the type of business and whether you are starting a business from scratch, or buying an existing business. Make sure you research your requirements before entering into any contracts or commitments. 

The funds required to start the business, are in addition, to the working capital needed to cover the day-to-day expenses once the business is operating.

Michael runs his educational consultancy business from a home office. Here is a list of some of his start-up costs:

  • registering an internet domain
  • hosting a website and email
  • marketing costs, for example, graphic design, business cards, advertisement
  • notebook computer
  • telephone
  • PO box.
 

Ahmed runs his retail outlet from a rented shop. Here is a list of some of his start-up costs.

  • shelves and fittings for shop
  • stock
  • bond and rent in advance on shop
  • business name registration
  • bond for electricity
  • telephone
  • personal computer.
 

Patty runs a painting and decorating business from home. Some of her start-up costs include:

  • personal protective equipment
  • mobile telephone
  • painting supplies and equipment
  • shed for storage of supplies and equipment
  • motor vehicle
  • newspaper advertisment.
