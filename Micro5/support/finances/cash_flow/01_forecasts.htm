Forecasting business activity

One of the most important aspects in running a business is having sufficient working capital to meet the day to day running costs of the business. 

The time taken for a new business to become profitable will vary depending on:

  • the type of business
  • number of competitors
  • economic and other environmental factors
  • marketing strategy of the owner.
 

It is essential to forecast the cash likely to come in and to leave the business. Businesses have closed down purely and simply because there were insufficient funds to keep operating.

If you're taking over an existing business, you can use previous figures to help with your forecasts. If you're starting a new business, you'll have to make realistic estimates.

Don't forget to take into account any seasonal fluctuations or trends for your industry that may affect future sales. For example, an ice-cream parlour is likely to experience quiet times in cooler weather if it sells only ice-cream.
