Debt and equity financing

Many people might prefer to use their own funds to start up a business. Using personal funds means there will be no loan repayments and no sharing the business with anyone else.

However, many people also don't have enough of their own money and need to look elsewhere for funds.

Equity financing

Equity financing is where money is exchanged for a share of the business.

Seed capitalists and 'business angels' that is, private investors looking for good business opportunities to invest in, are increasingly popular methods of obtaining finance.

This type of finance allows you to obtain funds generally without having to make repayments at specific times. The main disadvantage is that you will own less of your business and could lose some management control.

Debt financing

Debt financing means borrowing money which has to be repaid, with interest, over a period of time. There is a legal obligation to repay the loan and interest. Unlike equity finance, the lender doesn't gain an ownership in the business. The interest on the loan is generally tax deductible.

Getting advice

Financial brokers, solicitors and accountant can provide professional advice on the best financing option to meet your needs.
