Comparing loans

If you need to approach a financial institution for a loan, remember that the industry is competitive and you may be able to negotiate a good rate.

Rates

Are rates fixed? With fixed rate offers, the rate may be slightly higher than the variable rate offers, but may be a good option if rates are likely to rise. 

Establishment fees

Find out how much stamp duty is payable. Establishment fees are common and are designed to cover paperwork such as drawing up contracts and doing searches. Find out the exact amount and whether it is required up front or rolled into the loan.

Ongoing fees

Are there on-going fees, such as management or account keeping fees? Low interest rates may seem attractive but if there are high on-going fees, they could make the effective interest rate higher than other loans.

Interest free periods

Sometimes interest free or low interest loans are offered for particular time periods, for example, the first 12 months. Always make sure you check the fine print for any hidden charges or penalties.
