Case study - Ursula Dutkiewicz

Ursula has been a professional ceramic artist since completing a Bachelor of Fine Art at the Victorian College of the Arts in 1993. Ursula explored her business idea through the NEIS scheme and now operates a ceramic business from the Gasworks Art Park in Melbourne. She has created a range of art works including ceramic paintings, sculptures, functional wares and murals. 

Making a profitable business from your art requires passion, hard work and a social conscience. To develop her successful ceramic art business Ursula has worked in a variety of areas within the community.  Since 1993 Ursula has been involved in over 45 exhibitions.

She has received commissions to produce artworks for various organisations, implemented community arts projects and run ceramic workshops. Ursula has also branched out into public speaking and education, working in schools as an artist in residence, both in Australia and overseas.

Recent commissions include:

  • Travelling Art Installation - The Art of Suff-Rage
  • Brighton University (UK) School of Health Professions - Tree of Health mural.

Recent professional practice include:

  • Hunt Club Community Art Centre, Deer Park Pottery class for children
  • Ceramical - Joint Councils Access for All Abilities visual arts program for people with physical disabilities.

Recent exhibitions include:

  • Toyota Sculpture Exhibition, Toyota Spirit Gallery
  • British Sociologists Association, University of East London
  • Royal South Australian Society of Arts, Adelaide. 
 

Future business

Future business developments are focused on finding new opportunities and markets, while keeping artistic integrity. Ursula is considering taking up some international opportunities and continues to research new ideas through varied and interesting projects that inspire her and lead to creating art works in new and exciting directions.
