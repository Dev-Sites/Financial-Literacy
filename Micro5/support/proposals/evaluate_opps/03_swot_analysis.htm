SWOT analysis

SWOT is an acronym used to describe particular Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for a business.  

Conducting a SWOT analysis requires extensive research into the internal factors of the business, as well as the external environment in which it operates. Like any planning tool SWOT is only as good as the information it contains.Thorough research and accurate information are essential for the SWOT analysis to identify key factors for a business.

SWOT Matrix

When analysing SWOT information you have gathered, it is important to understand how these elements work together. When a business matches internal strengths to external opportunities, it meets the needs of its customers. In addition, a business should act to convert internal weaknesses into strengths and external threats into opportunities.

Questions to consider when researching information for your business SWOT analysis.

Assess your market

  • What is happening externally and internally that will affect your business?
  • Who are your customers?
  • What are the strengths and weaknesses of each of your competitors?
  • What are the driving forces behind sales trends?
  • What are important and potentially important markets?
  • What is happening in the world that might affect your business?
  • What does it take to be successful in this market? (List the strengths all companies need to compete successfully in this market.)

Assess your business

  • What does your business do best?
  • What are your business resources - assets, intellectual property, and people?
  • What are your key capabilities functions of the business?

Assess your competition

  • How are you different from the competition?
  • What are the general market conditions of your business?
  • What needs are there for your products and services?
  • What are the customer-market-technology opportunities?
  • What are the customer's problems and complaints with the current products and services available?
  • What "If only…." statements does a customer make?

The information collated for the SWOT analysis needs to be considered and then acted upon. To make effective improvements in your business:

  • focus on your strengths.
  • shore up your weaknesses.
  • capitalise on your opportunities.
  • recognise your threats.
