Business risks

Perceived risks can be internal or external to the business. 

Internal risks can be issues surrounding physical capability to perform tasks associated with the business such as:

  • having sufficient financial assets to commence and sustain trade
  • having access to appropriate staff / contractors
  • other internal risk areas such as licences for the business, appropriate location, and necessary skills.

With regard to external risks, a scan of the business environment is needed to understand whether the external forces are negative (as opposed to positive) from a business point of view.  These forces include:

  • demographics (population information)
  • economic environment
  • cultural and social trends
  • political and legal issues
  • technology
  • competition
  • pressure groups
  • the physical environment.
