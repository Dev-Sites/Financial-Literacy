Writing a business proposal

Starting your own micro business can open up exciting opportunities for you. It will take hard work so think long and hard about it.

With good research and planning your business idea can thrive. Being well prepared helps you develop a positive attitude and confidence. It enables you to deal with your weaknesses and encourage you to take the opportunity to pursue your micro business idea.

The following proposal outline will give you an idea of the areas you may include in your own micro business proposal.

Getting started

  • Before you start your own business, you need to work out if it will satisfy your wants and aspirations.
  • Think about the effects of running your own business and how it will affect your lifestyle and family.
  • Assess your own strengths and weaknesses and determine actions to eliminate any problems.

 

 
Business proposal Requirements

Title page
  • Business name
  • Address
  • Contact details
  • Date of preparation

Table of contents
  • Clear information listing the contents of your micro business proposal
  • Number all pages

Executive summary
  • Purpose of micro business proposal
  • Brief description of your business idea
    (Expressed in plain terms so it can be understood by a non-technical person)

Business aim
  • Your business mission
  • The customer needs that your business will seek to satisfy

Technical viability
  • Is the product or service ready for sale? If not, how far has it been developed?
  • The resources (plant, equipment, premises, material inputs, technology and skills) required to provide your product or service
  • List main suppliers
  • Regulatory standards or requirements that must be satisfied

Commercial viability
  • SWOT analysis of your micro business idea
  • Potential sales volume
  • How prices are determined in a business like yours
  • Your proposed pricing structure

Your industry
  • Description of the industry in which your business will operate
  • Trends and future prospects

Business administration
  • Key people involved
  • Roles, responsibilities and background
  • Skills
  • Formal qualifications required
  • Include resumes, where appropriate, in appendices section of plan
  • Key advisers to your business, for example, accountant, bank manager, NEIS trainer

Market research
    How you have carried out market research and description of market place
  • Describe potential customers and why they will buy your product or service
  • The number of potential customers and their location
  • How you will sell your product or service
  • List of competitors and SWOT analysis on competitors
  • Service and support you will provide to customers

Daily operations
    How daily business will be carried out
  • Equipment requirements
  • Quality control

Risks
    List the major risks that you face
  • How you propose to minimise the risks
  • How soon the business will be able to support you and how you propose to survive until then

Finance
    How much money you need to start up
  • How much money you have available to invest in the business
  • Funding mix, equity and borrowings
  • How funds will be used
  • Business viability and timeline to profitability
  • Break-even analysis
  • Financial projections
  • Cash flow analysis and the assumptions on which it is based
  • Estimate of fixed and variable expenses

Appendices
    Photos of products or service you will provide
  • Brochures
  • Summary of market research results
  • Resumes of key personnel
  • Letters of reference etc
