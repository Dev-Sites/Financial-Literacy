Develop a web site

Selling products or services online is almost mandatory for businesses today.  An e-commerce web site is simply an extension of your existing business. Apart from capturing new and untapped clients, when you go online it also allows your existing clients to browse your catalogue of products or services, select them and place them in a virtual shopping cart for later purchase. Look around the internet at great home pages and make your homepage equally as compelling.

Here's some advice to help you develop an attractive and usable web site, but make sure you consult a web design professional.

Homepage

Your homepage requires your logo to be prominently displayed in the top left area.

  • Your home page requires a link for email and your telephone number.
  • Contact information is critical to your site. It shouldn't be hidden on the 'contact us' page.
 

What is your business?

Include a statement that describes what you do or sell in the middle of your homepage.

 

Customers want to know:

  • Can I use your services?
  • Do you sell to my business or are you wholesale only?
  • How do I find out what products you sell and what they cost?
 

Finding information

Include a search field on your home page. It will help visitors find what they need.

 

The look

Keep it simple and consistent. Do not use neon colours or hard to read fonts.

 

Images

Be careful with the size of images used on your web site because the higher the resolution the longer they take to load.

 

Animation

Limit animations and make the site look sleek, professional and trustworthy.

 

How big is a web page?

Make the page flexible and wide enough for an average monitor (750 pixels).

 

Old information

If your homepage has news or upcoming events make sure that you regularly update the site.

 

Navigation

Navigation (links) should be clear, logical and intuitive.

  • If a visitor can't find what they want from your homepage, they will leave.
  • Write a simple outline of what pages you want on your site.
 

Wasting space

If you have nothing to say, delete that page from your site. You need concrete information that visitors and buyers want to read, but they will expect at least one fluffy page (usually 'About Us').

 

Search engines

Once your business web site is developed, advertise it so it shows up on at least one of the major search engines.
