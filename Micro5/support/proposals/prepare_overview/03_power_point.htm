Making a presentation

Following are key points to consider if you are developing a presentation using presentation software.

Presentation Design

  • Don't overload your slides with too much text or data.
  • Make sure you can read text from a distance.
  • Avoid using a large body of text.
  • Number your slides and give them a title.
  • Aim to use a few powerful slides.
  • Let the picture or graphic tell the story.
  • Proofread everything.
  • Prepare a company logo slide for your presentation.
    • You can add a logo and other graphics to every slide using the slide master feature.
 

Visual elements

  • A font size of 28 to 34 with a bold font is recommended for subtitles.
    • The title default size is 44.
    • Use a san serif font for titles.
  • Use clear, simple visuals.
  • Use contrast, light on dark or dark on light.
  • Place your graphics in a similar location within each screen.
 

Text

  • Font size must be large enough to be easily read.
    • Size 28 to 34 with a bold font is recommended.
  • Keep fonts consistent.
    • Do not use different fonts on every page.
  • Overuse of text is a common mistake.
    • Too much text makes the slide unreadable. Stick to a few key words. 
    • If your audience is reading the slides they are not paying attention to you. If possible, make your point with graphics instead of text.
 

Statistics

  • Don't compare thousands to millions.
  • For sales data stick to a single market in the presentation.
    • For example industry sales, company sales, or sales to a specific market segment are all different scales.They should not be mixed.
  • Cite your source on the same slide as the statistic, using a smaller font.
 

Charts

  • Charts need to be clearly labelled.
 

Backgrounds

  • Backgrounds should never distract from the presentation.
  • Using the default white background is hard on the viewer's eyes.
    • Light colored backgrounds with dark text, or vice versa, look good.
    • A dark background with white font reduces glare.
  • Colors appear lighter when projected. Pale colors often appear as white.
  • Consistent backgrounds add to a professional appearance.
  • For a long presentation, you may want to change background designs when shifting to a new topic.
 

Sound and movement

  • Use sounds and transition effects sparingly.
  • Animation effects can be interesting when used in moderation.
    • Too much animation is distracting.
  • You can insert video and audio clips if they are relevant.
  • You can also insert hyperlinks.
